Summary
This study not only reinforces existing knowledge about
individuals who span organizational boundaries but
provides a different approach in examining the prodﾂｭ
uct management system. Many more issues, however,
need to be studied. For example, it would be useful
to know how instrumental boundary spanning is in the
innovation process. Because the product manager aids
in the dissemination of information, he/she may be
facilitating transfer of new ideas and technology across
boundaries, thus stimulating the innovation process.
It is also possible that the product manager functions
as a gatekeeper of information in conducting boundﾂｭ
ary spanning. Other avenues for research include the
means by which the product manager can monitor the
environment, the information processing mechanisms
used, and how the successful product manager copes
with stress and conflict.
Since the product management system has scored
significant gains in administering complex product
lines, this research illuminates several behavioral asﾂｭ
pects of the system. Environmental change and turﾂｭ
bulence are likely to accelerate in the future; thus, adﾂｭ
ditional studies that examine the adaptation of various
product line administration systems to changing enﾂｭ
vironmental forces will be needed. This study is a step
in that direction.
