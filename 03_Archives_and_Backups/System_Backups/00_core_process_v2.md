<summary><h2>Coding Process Overview</h2></summary>

According to the manual, the coding process involves:
1. **Article Descriptors**: Recording basic inclusion/exclusion judgments and publication details.
2. **Study/Sample Descriptors**: Extracting demographic details (age, gender, tenure, occupation) and study design characteristics (cross-sectional vs. longitudinal).
3. **Measure Descriptors**: Noting specific details of the boundary spanning measures used (number of items, min/max scores, report types like self-rated vs. other-rated).
4. **Effect Sizes**: Recording the mean, standard deviation, reliability estimates (Cronbach's alpha), and the uncorrected Pearson product-moment correlation ($r$) between boundary spanning and other variables.
