ACKNOWLEDGEMENTS
I am deeply indebted to Diane F. Witmer, my advisor, for her patience, 
understanding, and knowledge base that extended beyond the realm of public relations 
and into other sectors or communication, particularly organizational communication. 
Thank you Diane for not only listening to my radical notions, but for providing me with 
the resources necessary to apply these ideas. Without your level of openness, I could not 
have developed this new perspective of public relations and completed my dissertation.
I am greatly appreciative of the committee's support for my dissertation. All of 
you have been instrumental in the development and completion of the study. Thank you 
Deborah Dillon, Dennis Mumby, and Marifran Mattson for your insightful comments all 
along and your constructive feedback on my prospectus. I am especially grateful for 
your high degree of patience.
