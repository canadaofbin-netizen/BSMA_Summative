4.2 Common method bias (CMB)
CMB is a systematic error that occurs when measuring constructs and is the bias that
appears when measuring two or more variables using the same method (Billings and
Wroten, 1978; Podsakoff et al., 2003). Covariance occurs due to a similar procedure when
measuring two or more variables using the same method. When a correlation between the
measurement methods exists, it is difficult to be certain of the correlation between the
measurement variables, even if validity exists (Podsakoff and Organ, 1986).
