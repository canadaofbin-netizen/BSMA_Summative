Open innovation is crucial for achieving high-quality and sustainable business growth. However, it remains 
unclear how prospective boundary-spanning search and responsive boundary-spanning search impact organi -
zational resilience and open innovation in the VUCA (Volatility, Uncertainty, Complexity, and Ambiguity) new 
normal. Based on the resource-based theory and dynamic capabilities theory, this paper classifies boundary- 
spanning search into prospective boundary-spanning search and responsive boundary-spanning search to in-
vestigates the impact on open innovation and organizational resilience, and analyze the moderating role of big 
data analytics capability. The empirical study of 293 Chinese smart manufacturing firms indicates that both 
prospective and responsive boundary-spanning search have an inverted U-shaped impact on open innovation and 
organizational resilience. Organizational resilience partially mediates the relationships between prospective 
boundary-spanning search, responsive boundary-spanning search, and open innovation. Moreover, big data 
analytics capability moderates and flattens the inverted U-shaped relationships between prospective and 
responsive boundary-spanning search, and organizational resilience. This study explores the intrinsic mechanism 
of prospective and responsive boundary-spanning search on open innovation through organizational resilience. 
The findings enrich and expand the research on the antecedents of open innovation.
1.Introduction
In the context of the new normal characterized by Volatility, Un-
certainty, Complexity, and Ambiguity (VUCA) (Martín-Rojas et al., 
2023 ), firms face the imperative to adapt to dynamic environments and 
engage in continuous exploration and application of new knowledge 
through open innovation (Martín-Rojas et al., 2023 ). Open innovation 
refers to the firm’s use of both internal and external complementary 
innovation resources to achieve innovation in the innovation process 
(Naqshbandi and Tabche, 2018 ; Sims et al., 2019 ). The essence of open 
innovation is that the firm breaks through the original organizational 
boundaries, conducts extensive and in-depth scanning and searching of 
the external environment in which it is located, and emphasizes the 
integration and utilization of both internal and external innovation re-
sources of the firm. Compared to closed innovation, open innovation emphasizes collaboration and sharing, aiming to break down traditional 
barriers and obstacles in innovation models (Chesbrough, 2003 ; West 
and Gallagher, 2006 ). External innovation forces can be brought in to 
accelerate the pace and enhance efficiency. Exposure to a wider range of 
innovation sources can also increase the diversity and possibilities of 
internal innovation (West et al., 2014 ; West and Gallagher, 2006 ). 
Simultaneously, open innovation assists firms in adapting to market 
changes by maintaining close contact with consumers, suppliers, and 
other stakeholders (Hung and Chou, 2013 ; Lopez-Vega et al., 2016 ; 
Wang et al., 2023 ; West and Bogers, 2014 ). This enables firms to quickly 
learn about market needs and trends, allowing them to adjust the di-
rection of innovation and ensure that their products meet market de-
mands. Thus, firms engaged in open innovation cannot rely solely on 
their existing knowledge to meet the demands of innovation; but must 
also tap into external knowledge resources (Garriga et al., 2013 ; Qu

Resource-based theory posits that unique and valuable knowledge is 
a crucial resource for firms to gain a competitive edge (Chadwick et al., 
2015 ; Zhu et al., 2023 ). To infuse their operations with innovative ele-
ments, firms need to capture heterogeneous knowledge keenly (Sirmon 
et al., 2011 ). Boundary-spanning search represents a knowledge-seeking 
activity that firms undertake across existing organizational or techno -
logical boundaries (Yang et al., 2021 ; Zhu et al., 2023 ). 
Boundary-spanning search is an important means for firms to acquire 
heterogeneous knowledge and overcome internal rigidities and capa-
bility traps, facilitating the establishment of innovation advantages (Zhu 
et al., 2023 ). Proactive boundary-spanning search represents a leading 
method for anticipating and preparing for future changes and trends. In 
contrast, a reactive, passive search, also known as a responsive 
boundary-spanning search, is designed to gather and analyze informa -
tion when changes in the external environment occur or when events 
have already had an impact (Yang et al., 2021 ). Previous studies have 
analyzed the significant role of external knowledge acquisition in 
innovation (Cammarano et al., 2019 ; Houessou et al., 2023 ; Wang et al., 
2020 ). However, few have explored the relationship between 
boundary-spanning search, specifically prospective and responsive 
boundary-spanning search, and open innovation in terms of search 
timing. Therefore, investigating the impact of prospective and respon -
sive boundary-spanning search on firms’ open innovation is a worth -
while endeavor.
Furthermore, the survival and prosperity of an organization depends 
heavily on its ability to adapt to a dynamic environment (Dubey et al., 
2023 ). The resource-based theory places considerable emphasis on the 
role of resources in fostering innovation (Martín-Rojas et al., 2023 ). 
However, it lacks a clear articulation of the specific mechanisms through 
which resources drive innovation (Teece et al., 1997 ). As an extension of 
resource-based theory, dynamic capabilities theory indicates that firms’ 
innovations are contingent upon not only the resources themselves, but 
also their dynamic capabilities (Martín-Rojas et al., 2023 ). The core of 
dynamic capabilities theory is to focus on how firms can adapt and 
optimize resources allocation to achieve innovation in a changing 
environment (Teece et al., 1997 ). Dynamic capabilities theory suggests 
that in a turbulent external environment, if the resources that previously 
provided the firm with a competitive advantage prove difficult to adapt 
to changing environmental demands, it will lead to core rigidity and 
impede the sustainable development of the firm (Dubey et al., 2023 ; 
Khlystova et al., 2022 ; Napier et al., 2024 ). Organizations face contin -
uous shocks from the external environment during their operations, 
requiring organizational resilience to effectively manage complex and 
evolving contingencies (Lin and Fan, 2024 ). Organizational resilience, 
as a dynamic capability, enables firms to efficiently and flexibly manage 
external knowledge, anticipate potential threats, and respond effectively 
to risks that may arise in the process of open innovation (Martín-Rojas 
et al., 2023 ). Previous research on the factors influencing organizational 
resilience has mainly focused on leadership characteristics (Tuazon 
et al., 2021 ), organizational resources (Do et al., 2022 ). Organizational 
resilience relies heavily on external knowledge, and a wealth of external 
knowledge enables organizations to quickly identify and respond to 
potential crises, facilitating their rapid recovery, rebound, and even 
reversal (Dan et al., 2017 ; Napier et al., 2024 ). Consequently, it is 
necessary to investigate the mechanism of prospective and responsive 
boundary-spanning search on organizational resilience. In addition, 
organizational resilience, as the dynamic ability of organizations to 
withstand, recover and grow from external shocks, also plays a key role 
in fostering open innovation (Hillmann and Guenther, 2021 ). Therefore, 
it is imperative to elucidate the influence of prospective and responsive 
boundary-spanning search on organizational resilience and explore its 
mediating role between prospective and responsive boundary-spanning 
search and open innovation.
In today’s highly competitive and uncertain business environment, 
the dynamic capabilities theory provides a powerful framework for understanding organizational resilience. Dynamic capabilities refer to a 
firm’s ability to sense, capture, and allocate internal and external re-
sources to respond to rapidly changing environments (Teece et al., 
1997 ). Meanwhile, prospective boundary-spanning search and respon -
sive boundary-spanning search are strategies for firms to achieve orga-
nizational resilience. Prospective boundary-spanning search enables 
firms to proactively cross organizational boundaries to explore new 
technologies and market trends in a wider range of areas (Dan et al., 
2017 ). This proactive exploration allows firms to proactively perceive 
opportunities and threats that may affect their development 
(Lopez-Vega et al., 2016 ). For example, BYD Auto foresaw the rise of the 
new-energy vehicle market through prospective boundary-spanning 
search. It established close cooperation with battery suppliers, devel -
oped battery R&D and production plans in advance, and attracted 
relevant technical talents to join the firm. This integration of internal 
and external resources effectively enhanced its competitiveness in the 
new-energy vehicles market and its ability to cope with market changes. 
Responsive boundary-spanning search enables firms to pay close 
attention to the dynamics of competitors and industry leaders and access 
timely their experiences of success and failure in technological innova -
tion, market strategy, etc. (Yang et al., 2021 ). When a firm discovers that 
a competitor launches a new product or adopts a new business model, it 
can expedite, learn from, and draw on its useful elements and avoid 
taking a detour in its own development process. For instance, Xiaomi’s 
drew on Apple’s industrial design concepts and Samsung’s supply chain 
management model. It launched cost - effective mobile phone models 
like the Redmi series and made localized improvements to meet the 
demands of China’s sinking market. By real-time monitoring the product 
iteration speed of competitors such as Huawei and OPPO, Xiaomi 
shortened the industry’s average 18-month R&D cycle to 9 months and 
rapidly launched benchmark-setting models. Through responsive 
boundary-spanning search strategy, Xiaomi witnessed a remarkable 
growth in its mobile phone market share in India, surpassing Samsung to 
become the top-selling brand.
The tenets of dynamic capabilities theory posit that firms engage in 
the construction, integration, and allocation of internal and external 
resources in a dynamic environment with the objective of coping with 
changes in the external environment (Ferreira et al., 2020 ; Napier et al., 
2024 ; Teece et al., 1997 ). In an evolving external market environment 
and dynamic internal organizational process, the firms dynamic capa-
bility to effectively respond to changes in the internal and external en-
vironments and resource consumption is the key factor determining its 
competitive advantage (Napier et al., 2024 ). In the context of digital 
economy, big data has emerged as a strategic resource and valuable 
information asset that enhances open innovation capability of firms 
(El-Kassar and Singh, 2019 ; Gupta and George, 2016 ; Shamim et al., 
2020 ). Effective resource management is essential for the survival and 
development of organizations operating in dynamic environments 
(Eisenhardt and Martin, 2000 ; Mikalef et al., 2019 ). While resource 
management provides a robust foundation for organizational opera -
tions, the accelerated evolution of information technology has precipi -
tated a correspondingly rapid industrial iteration and the emergence of a 
vast quantity of data (Huynh et al., 2023 ). This has rendered traditional 
resource management techniques insufficient for navigating the intri-
cate and dynamically evolving market landscape (Zhang et al., 2021 ). 
The value of big data presents a significant window of opportunity, and 
the ability of firms to rapidly identify, analyze, and process big data 
becomes a powerful adjunct to traditional resource management (Wang 
et al., 2020 ). Big data analytics capability is used to describe an orga-
nization’s ability to effectively collect, store, process, analyze, and 
extract valuable information from large, diverse, complex, and rapidly 
generated data (Huynh et al., 2023 ). This capability is an essential 
component of dynamic capability that encompasses not only the mastery 
of data technology but also a profound comprehension of data, utiliza -
tion of analytical instruments, generation of insights, and the capacity to 
make data-driven decisions (Wang et al., 2020 ). Big data analytics S. lytics have greater potential to transform external knowledge resources 
obtained through boundary-spanning search, fostering organizational 
resilience (Appiah, 2024 ; Xue and Wang, 2024 ). Thus, it is necessary to 
investigate the role of big data analytics capability and uncover their 
moderating effect on the relationship between prospective and respon -
sive boundary-spanning search and organizational resilience.
This paper employs resource-based theory and dynamic capabilities 
theory to examine how prospective and responsive boundary-spanning 
search enhances organizational resilience and promotes open innova -
tion. This study aims to highlight the importance of organizational 
resilience and big data analytics capability in fostering open innovation, 
while also considering the mediating role of the former and the 
moderating role of the latter. To achieve this goal, this study conducted 
empirical research in China’s smart manufacturing firms using a ques-
tionnaire survey. By uncovering the underlying mechanism through 
which prospective and responsive boundary-spanning search influences 
firm open innovation, this study contributes to the existing research on 
the antecedents of open innovation. Moreover, this research provides a 
strong theoretical foundation for firms to engage in open innovation.
The remainder of the paper is structured as follows: Section 2pre-
sents a theoretical model that offers a plausible account of the rela-
tionship between prospective and responsive boundary-spanning 
search, organizational resilience, big data analytics capability, and open 
innovation. Section 3delineates the methodology employed to evaluate 
the hypotheses. Section 4presents the results of the analysis. Section 5
presents the discussions, theoretical implications, managerial implica -
tions and future research.
2.Theoretical background and hypotheses
2.1. Boundary-spanning search and open innovation
The main idea behind open innovation is to efficiently combine in-
ternal and external innovation resources of firms, and accelerate 
deployment of technologies to market through collaborative partner -
ships and market channels (Hung and Chou, 2013 ; Wang et al., 2023 ; 
West and Bogers, 2014 ). Boundary-spanning search, which involves 
acquiring knowledge across organizational boundaries, is crucial for 
open innovation (Dan et al., 2017 ; Ogink et al., 2023 ). 
Boundary-spanning search can be categorized into two types: prospec -
tive and responsive boundary-spanning search (Yang et al., 2021 ; Zhu 
et al., 2023 ).Prospective boundary-spanning search enables firms to stay ahead of 
their competitors and actively explore knowledge resources that have 
not yet been widely accepted in the market but have potential value 
(Dan et al., 2017 ; Lopez-Vega et al., 2016 ). As previously observed by 
Singh and Fleming (2010) and McDonald and Eisenhardt (2019) , the 
prioritization of access to resources has the potential to enhance the 
diversity and novelty of a firm’s resource pool, thereby affording firms 
greater flexibility to innovate. Through prospective boundary-spanning 
search, firms can take the lead in realizing technological advancement 
and obtaining a first-mover advantage (Garriga et al., 2013 ). Therefore, 
prospective boundary-spanning search not only helps firms to improve 
learning efficiency and shorten the learning curve, but also breaks 
through their own resources and technological bottlenecks to explore 
the potential needs of users and future market opportunities 
(Chesbrough and Brunswicker, 2014 ; Katila and Chen, 2008 ). More 
importantly, prospective boundary-spanning search reduces the likeli -
hood of competitors taking a first-mover advantage and provides strong 
support for firms to establish unique competitive advantages. However, 
it is important to note that excessive prospective boundary-spanning 
search may have a negative impact on open innovation (Chunhsien 
et al., 2020 ). The hidden nature, complexity, and uncertainty of external 
knowledge make search difficult and may incur opportunity costs, i.e. 
distract firms (Dahlander et al., 2016 ; Khadijeh et al., 2020 ). For pro-
spective boundary-spanning search, excess search may result in knowl -
edge that is too complex and novel to be integrated with existing 
knowledge, leading to blocked innovation (Flor et al., 2018 ; Wang et al., 
2023 ). Given that firms have limited attention spans, excessive 
complexity can result in redundant knowledge within a firm, leading to 
wasted resources and hindering the conversion of new knowledge into 
innovation value. Therefore, we propose the following hypotheses. 
H1a. The impact of prospective boundary-spanning search on open 
innovation follows an inverted U-shaped curve.
In contrast, responsive boundary-spanning search enables firms to 
follow their competitors and search for knowledge resources already 
available in the market. As Jeong and Kim’s (2019) research indicates, 
responsive boundary-spanning search that adhere to established tech-
nological trajectories are conducive to enhancing the legitimacy of 
firms, facilitating their access to external heterogeneous resources in a 
more seamless manner and enabling them to innovate. Thus, responsive 
boundary-spanning search helps firms respond to market changes in a 
timely manner and reduces innovation risks. Through responsive 
boundary-spanning search, firms can obtain knowledge that is more in 
line with their own knowledge base, form new knowledge portfolios, 
and promote open innovation (Yang et al., 2021 ). At the same time, it 
can help firms identify knowledge to improve existing products or 
processes and increase customer recognition and acceptance, thereby 
facilitating open innovation. As responsive boundary-spanning search 
deepens, firms will encounter challenges associated with cognitive ri-
gidity and resources homogenization. In this way, firms will overly rely 
on established cognitive patterns and ways of thinking, as well as 
competitors’ practices, while underestimating the distinctive qualities 
and potential of innovations. This may impede the flow of open inno-
vation. Additionally, excessive responsive boundary-spanning search 
can lead to information overload and increased costs, thus negatively 
impacting open innovation. Moreover, excessive external information 
can hinder rational resource allocation of firms, leading to neglect of key 
knowledge integration and application, and missing opportunities for 
innovation. Therefore, we propose the following hypotheses. 
H1b. The impact of responsive boundary-spanning search on open 
innovation follows an inverted U-shaped curve.

ment in response to internal and external disturbances and threats 
(Parker and Ameen, 2018 ). It has become a crucial factor in a firm’s 
stable development in a complex and ever-changing business environ -
ment. Dynamic capabilities theory suggests that firms can develop new 
capabilities by effectively acquiring and integrating internal and 
external resources to better adapt to environmental changes and achieve 
their development goals (Xue and Wang, 2024 ). Therefore, in a business 
environment full of uncertainty, firms can enhance organizational 
resilience by responding effectively to various challenges.
Prospective boundary-spanning search is a proactive activity that 
encourages firms to cross organization boundaries and access hetero -
geneous information or resources that are not yet widely accepted 
(Katila and Chen, 2008 ; Yang et al., 2021 ). Accessing to valuable re-
sources is a necessary but not a sufficient condition for firms to establish 
an innovative advantage. Dynamic capabilities theory underscores the 
significance of firms integrating and leveraging resources. On the one 
hand, prospective boundary-spanning search can broaden a firm’s 
development thinking and effectively alleviates internal bottlenecks and 
strategic crises, and facilitate firms to increase their knowledge breadth 
and anticipate external shocks. In this way, firms can formulate corre -
sponding coping strategies (Dan et al., 2017 ). On the other hand, pro-
spective boundary-spanning search encourages firms to establish a close 
cooperation network with universities and other leading research in-
stitutions. This helps to update existing knowledge base of the firm and 
enhances organizational resilience, making it more capable of coping 
with external shocks and breaking organizational rigidity (Tasic et al., 
2020 ). However, it is important to note that when boundary-spanning 
search exceeds a certain level, firms may face problems such as infor-
mation overload and increased difficulty in knowledge integration 
(Dahlander et al., 2016 ). These problems can lead to less efficient 
organizational decision-making and slower response times, which in 
turn weakens organizational resilience (Wang et al., 2023 ). In the case of 
prospective boundary-spanning search, lack of experience may result in 
unnecessary resource development waste and increased potential risks 
and failures due to excessive search activities. Additionally, overly 
complex external networks may consume too much time and effort, thus 
affecting firms ’ ability to adapt and recover quickly. 
H2a.The effect of prospective boundary-spanning search on organi -
zational resilience follows an inverted U-shaped curve.
At the same time, responsive boundary-spanning search aims to 
deepen firms ’ understanding and utilization of their existing knowledge 
base (Yang et al., 2021 ). Research has shown that increased depth of 
knowledge significantly improves organizational resilience, enabling 
firms to better cope with challenges (Wang et al., 2023 ). Responsive 
boundary-spanning search enables firms to learn from their partners ’ 
successes in dealing with past external shocks, to strengthen internal 
cross-functional communication and collaboration based on those ex-
periences, enables firms to break down vertical and horizontal bound -
aries, and to strengthen organizational resilience. This way enables firms 
to reduce operating costs and strengthens their ability to respond 
(Parker and Ameen, 2018 ). However, firms that rely too heavily on 
responsive boundary-spanning search and neglect prospective 
boundary-spanning search may struggle to adapt to the complex and 
ever-changing external environment (Ferreras-M ˘endez et al., 2016 ). 
Over-reliance on responsive boundary-spanning search can result in 
missed opportunities for proactive adjustment and optimization, and 
increase the knowledge gap. Additionally, excessive pursuit of respon -
sive boundary-spanning search tends to limit the scope of firms, 
resulting in the acquisition of largely homogenized resources that lack 
uniqueness and heterogeneity. The redundancy of resources and the 
paucity of heterogeneous resources can easily lead to cognitive myopia. 
In other words, excessive responsive boundary-spanning search can lead 
to high levels of knowledge homogenization, which may increase the 
cost of knowledge integration, reduce cooperation efficiency, and generate redundant knowledge that could decrease firms ’ sensitivity to 
external threats. Therefore, we propose the following hypotheses. 
H2b.The effect of responsive boundary-spanning search on organi -
zational resilience follows an inverted U-shaped curve.
2.3. Organizational resilience and open innovation
The core of dynamic capabilities theory is that firms not only need to 
have a good resource base, but also need to have the ability to quickly 
perceive opportunities and threats, seize opportunities, and reconfigure 
resources in a timely manner (Schilke, 2014 ; Teece et al., 1997 ). Orga-
nizational resilience refers to the ability of an organization to quickly 
adapt, recover, and rebound in response to internal and external shocks 
or changes (Hung and Chou, 2013 ; Ogink et al., 2023 ). This ability en-
ables firms to maintain flexibility and stability in uncertain environ -
ments while also capitalize on new opportunities through learning and 
innovative development (Kantur and Iseri-Say, 2015 ).
In uncertain environments, external shocks often manifest as random 
and unpredictable changes (Chakrabarti, 2015 ). These shocks may 
originate from sudden crisis, market fluctuation, policy changes, eco-
nomic crisis or technological disruption, among others (Li et al., 2025 ). 
Shocks in uncertain environments place higher demands on organiza -
tional resilience, as firms need to be flexible in adjusting their resources 
and coping strategies in unknown directions (Napier et al., 2024 ).

cooperation and innovation in the face of failure, thereby ensuring the 
continued advancement of open innovation. Organizational resilience 
enables firms to integrate newly acquired external knowledge with in-
ternal resources, and breaks down barriers of traditional innovation 
models, thus facilitating open innovation (West and Bogers, 2014 ). In 
summary, organizational resilience is a key enabler of open innovation 
by fostering adaptability, enhancing resource integration, encouraging 
collaboration, and ensuring continuity in the face of challenges.

H3. The effect of organizational resilience on open innovation is 
positive.
2.4. The mediating role of organizational resilience
Dynamic capabilities theory posits that simply acquiring external 
resources is insufficient for firms to innovate effectively. Adapting to 
changes in the external environment is also a necessary condition for 
innovation. YahiaMarzouk and Jin (2022) highlighted the importance of 
resource acquisition in the innovation process of firms, as well as the 
difficulty of achieving organizational strategic goals if these resources 
are not effectively utilized. Meanwhile, the dynamic capabilities theory 
posits that the perceived capability, integration capability, and coordi -
nation capability of a firm are crucial elements that comprise its dy-
namic capabilities. Organizational resilience can be defined as the 
capacity of the firm to perceive and adapt to environmental changes, 
coordinate internal and external resources to withstand external shocks, 
and learn from lessons to achieve rebound and recovery. Accordingly, 
organizational resilience represents a crucial element of the firm’s dy-
namic capabilities and a key driver for achieving open innovation. We 
thus argue that organizational resilience mediates the relationship be-
tween boundary-spanning search and open innovation, as appropriate 
boundary-spanning search facilitates the formation of organizational 
resilience, which is a key component of open innovation.
Prospective boundary-spanning search is a crucial way for firms to 
access a diverse knowledge base by interacting across boundaries. This 
contributes to accessing heterogeneous resources (Lopez-Vega et al., 
2016 ), which in turn compensates for the lack of internal innovation 
resources and enhances the firm’s ability to cope with market changes 
and foster organizational resilience (Wang et al., 2023 ). Prospective 
boundary-spanning search not only is beneficial for firms to search tacit, 
irreplaceable, and potentially valuable knowledge (Yang et al., 2021 ), 
but also can help firms obtain more innovation resources, reduce the risk 
of knowledge inertia, and enhance the adaptability of firms to the external environment. Accordingly, firms can quickly adjust their stra-
tegies and processes in the face of external turbulence and threats, 
enhancing organizational resilience (Napier et al., 2024 ). This resilience 
ensures the firm’s ability to remain stable and sustainable in the face of 
external shocks (Lin and Fan, 2024 ; Xue and Wang, 2024 ). By combining 
search opportunities with open innovation strategies, firms can achieve 
their innovation goals. Furthermore, prospective boundary-spanning 
search can be more effective in identifying external market and envi-
ronmental changes, allowing firms to access information ahead of 
competitors (Wang et al., 2023 ). This increased environmental sensi-
tivity and predictive power can enhance organizational resilience and 
facilitate the identification of potential collaborators for obtaining re-
sources, information, and support to jointly advance the innovation 
process.
Responsive boundary-spanning search focuses on finding comple -
mentary knowledge resources that match the original knowledge base 
and are readily available, deepening the rational allocation and utili-
zation of existing knowledge by the firm (Yang et al., 2021 ). Organi -
zational resilience ensures that firms maintain stable operations by 
adapting to industry trends or competing with rivals, leveraging accu-
mulated knowledge resources, managing redundant information, 
improving the efficiency of knowledge and resource utilization, and 
ultimately fostering open innovation. In this way, organizational resil-
ience enables firms to identify warning signs, re-evaluate past successes 
and failures of stakeholders (Hillmann and Guenther, 2021 ; Lin and Fan, 
2024 ), utilize partner experiences to adjust product solutions and pro-
cesses in a timely manner, and achieve long-term development goals 
through open innovation. Therefore, we propose the following 
hypotheses. 
H4a. Organizational resilience serves as a mediator in the relationship 
between prospective boundary-spanning search and open innovation.
H4b. Organizational resilience serves as a mediator in the relationship 
between responsive boundary-spanning search and open innovation.
2.5. The moderating role of big data analytics capability
Dynamic capabilities theory emphasizes the need for firms to possess 
the ability to adapt and learn dynamically in response to environmental 
changes, including insight, prediction, and feedback (Teece et al., 1997 ). 
Insight refers to a firm’s capacity to integrate internal and external data 
to deepen its understanding of market trends, customer needs, and the 
potential of internal resources (Eisenhardt and Martin, 2000 ). Prediction 
involves firms using big data analytics to build predictive models, 
identify potential opportunities and risks, and formulate response stra-
tegies in advance (Gupta and George, 2016 ). Feedback means continu -
ously monitoring market dynamics and making timely strategy 
adjustments through big data analytics to enhance the firm’s adapt -
ability speed and flexibility. From this, it can be seen that dynamic ca-
pabilities theory regards big data as a valuable organizational resource. 
The capability to analyze big data is an organizational capability based 
on data analytics. It enables firms to analyze generic knowledge from a 
vast volume of big data and transforms it into specific knowledge re-
sources required for firms’ innovation. In contrast to general dynamic 
capabilities, big data analytics capability aims to enhance an organiza -
tion’s competitive advantage by providing new insights. This is not an 
ad-hoc practice but rather a continuous process of adaptation and 
change. Organizations can acquire external heterogeneous resources, 
which help them anticipate change and proactively adapt to uncer -
tainty. Big data analytics capability refers to the exclusive insights 
derived from capturing, storing, and analyzing data through the efficient 
utilization of technology and talent within an organization (Ferraris 
et al., 2019 ; Huynh et al., 2023 ; Mikalef et al., 2019 ). In the digital 
economy era, big data analytics capability is not only a matter of tech-
nical proficiency, but also encompasses the capability to manage vast 
data assets and synchronize operational endeavors (AlNuaimi et al., S. Jin et al.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       Technovation  145 (2025)  103273  
5 2021 ). Consequently, we posit that organizational resilience is not solely 
influenced by boundary-spanning search, but also significantly depends 
on a firm’s big data analytics capability. Additionally, big data analytics 
capability serves as a moderating factor in the inverted U-shaped rela-
tionship between boundary-spanning search and organizational 
resilience.
Specifically, when firms possess stronger big data analytics capa-
bility, they are able to more precisely identify potential prospective 
boundary-spanning search opportunities through deep data mining and 
meticulous interpretation of vast amounts of information. This capa-
bility enhances firms to effectively integrate and utilize external 
knowledge resources (Cadden et al., 2023 ; Huynh et al., 2023 ; Sivarajah 
et al., 2024 ), thereby shortening learning curve, mitigating external 
uncertainties, and fostering organizational resilience. Furthermore, 
stronger big data analytics capability allows firms to monitor and 
accurately predict the risks associated with prospective 
boundary-spanning search activities in real-time. As a result, firms are 
better able to assess the risk level and modify appropriate response 
strategies for prospective boundary-spanning search, averting undue 
risks and maintaining risk levels within manageable boundaries. 
Consequently, the negative impact of excessive prospective 
boundary-spanning search on organizational resilience is mitigated. 
Conversely, when firms have a low level of big data analytics capability, 
they may struggle to effectively distill and integrate new knowledge 
gained through prospective boundary-spanning search. This can lead to 
slower knowledge acquisition, poor knowledge internalization, and a 
higher risk of knowledge misapplication, ultimately affecting organi -
zational adaptability and undermining resilience (Cadden et al., 2023 ; 
Rialti et al., 2019 ). Additionally, without robust data support, 
cross-sector collaboration and decision-making processes may become 
overly reliant on subjective judgments and past experiences, leading to 
inefficient coordination and challenges in managing cultural conflicts 
(AlNuaimi et al., 2021 ; Gupta and George, 2016 ). This can increase in-
ternal chaos and further weaken organizational resilience. Therefore, a 
low level of big data analytics capability not only diminishes the positive 
impact of prospective boundary-spanning search on organizational 
resilience but also exacerbates the negative consequences of excessive 
prospective boundary-spanning search.
With a high level of big data analytics capability, firms are able to 
capture market changes in real-time, enabling them to swiftly formulate 
(Mikalef et al., 2019 ; Sivarajah et al., 2024 ), and adjust strategies in 
response to responsive boundary-spanning search. This confers a high 
level of responsiveness and adaptability, which significantly enhances 
organizational resilience. Furthermore, firms with stronger big data 
analytics capability can precisely identify boundary-spanning targets, 
thus avoid blind trends and efficiently allocating resources. Accordingly, 
the negative consequences of resource fragmentation on organizational 
resilience will be prevented. In essence, a high level of big data analytics 
capability amplifies the positive impact of responsive 
boundary-spanning search on organizational resilience and mitigates 
the negative effects of excessive responsive boundary-spanning search. 
Instead, a low level of big data analytics capability can lead to slow 
sensing of market changes and competitive dynamics (Cadden et al., 
2023 ; Lin and Fan, 2024 ; Rialti et al., 2019 ). This slows down firms’ 
response speed, making firms prone to imprecise responsive 
boundary-spanning search, potentially missing critical timing or 
responding inappropriately. Additionally, inadequate big data analytics 
capability restricts firms’ ability to efficiently extract knowledge from 
vast heterogeneous sources. This inefficient knowledge management in 
further undermines the application of sensing new knowledge and 
business renewal, thereby weakening organizational resilience. Conse -
quently, low level of big data analytics capability not only reduces the 
positive impact of responsive boundary-spanning search on organiza -
tional resilience but also exacerbates the negative consequences of 
excessive responsive boundary-spanning search. Therefore, we propose 
the following hypotheses. H5a. Big data analytics capability serves as a moderator in the rela-
tionship between prospective boundary-spanning search and organiza -
tional resilience.
H5b. Big data analytics capability serves as a moderator in the rela-
tionship between responsive boundary-spanning search and organiza -
tional resilience. Fig. 1shows theoretical model of our research.
3.Methods
3.1. Data and samples
We utilized a questionnaire to collect data. To establish the target 
audience for our questionnaire, we referenced the classification of the 
smart manufacturing industry outlined by the National Bureau of Sta-
tistics within their Classification of China’s Strategic Emerging In-
dustries. Smart manufacturing, an industry heavily reliant on 
knowledge, maintains close communication and cooperation with 
external stakeholders. Therefore, our survey targets encompassed 
several subdomains within smart manufacturing, including new energy 
vehicles, high-end equipment manufacturing, new materials, artificial 
intelligence, and biopharmaceuticals. China is a major global 
manufacturing center with rapid growth and large - scale smart 
manufacturing industry. It boasts abundant firm cases and data re-
sources, which provide an adequate sample base for our study. Smart 
manufacturing firms are highly reliant on technological innovation and 
cross-border cooperation. In the face of technological changes and 
market competition, they frequently engage in boundary-spanning 
search activities, which makes the study results more typical and 
representative. As significant proponents of data-driven transformation 
and innovation in China, smart manufacturing firms achieve compre -
hensive intelligence in production, management, and services through 
utilizing advanced technologies, including modern information tech-
nology and intelligent equipment. Smart manufacturing companies are 
typically situated at the vanguard of technological advancement, con-
fronting rapidly evolving market conditions and technological de-
velopments. In order to maintain their competitive advantage, it is 
necessary for these firms to conduct constant boundary-spanning 
searches to identify new market opportunities, potential threats, and 
competitive dynamics. In light of the above situation, the investigation 
into the activities of smart manufacturing firms offers insights into the 
function of prospective and responsive boundary-spanning search in the 
context of open innovation. Furthermore, smart manufacturing firms 
possess a substantial quantity of data resources, which can be leveraged 
to achieve comprehensive monitoring and optimization of the produc -
tion process through big data analytics. The richness and analyzability of 
such data provide invaluable resources for studying firms’ open 
innovation.
In order to obtain representative sample data, this paper combines 
the distribution characteristics of smart manufacturing firms and the 
focus of China’s regional economic development. The sample is 
comprised of middle and senior managers from firms in the key regions 
of Beijing, Shanghai, Zhengzhou, Guangdong, Chongqing, and Xi’an. 
Specifically, Beijing is the capital of China, with a relatively complete 
industrial system and a high level of economic development. Shanghai is 
one of the pioneering demonstration zones of China’s reform and 
opening-up, and also one of the most economically developed and 
innovative regions in China. Zhengzhou is one of the densely populated 
regions in China, with relatively balanced economic development, a 
certain industrial base and human resources. Guangzhou is one of the 
most developed and dynamic regions in the Chinese economy, with a 
certain level of industrial development and human resources. Guangz -
hou is one of the most economically developed and dynamic regions in 
China, with a well-developed industrial system and an advanced 
manufacturing industry. Chongqing is an important strategic support 
point for the development of China’s western region, with a solid S. from foreign literature, ensuring the accuracy of our expressions. 
Additionally, we invited two experts in the field of innovation to review

items. Their suggestions also guided us in adjusting the item order. Prior

questionnaire.

exclude missing values, multiple-choice answers, and randomly filled

3.3. Common variance bias
We employed three methods to assess the potential impact of com-
mon variance biases (Hair et al., 2011 ; Podsakoff and Organ, 1986 ). 
Firstly, we measured each variable through multiple question items in 
our questionnaire design, ensuring that antecedent and outcome vari-
ables were placed in distinct sections. Secondly, we conducted a Har-
man ’s one-factor test to determine the extent of common variance bias. 
The results revealed that the first factor accounted for 39.787 % of the 
total variance, falling below the 50 % threshold. This indicates that the 
issue of common variance bias in our study is not significant. Thirdly, we 
tested the fit of the one-factor model using confirmatory factor analysis. 
The results demonstrated poor model fit. This further corroborates our 
findings and confirms that there are no serious common variance bias 
issues in our sample data.
3.4. Reliability and validity
Reliability test is typically evaluated by using two key coefficients: 
the composite reliability (CR) coefficient and the internal consistency 
reliability coefficient (Cronbach ’s α coefficient). Prior to calculating the 
CR coefficients and Cronbach ’s α for prospective boundary-spanning 
search, responsive boundary-spanning search, organizational resil-
ience, big data analytics capability, and open innovation, confirmatory 
factor analysis was conducted for each research variable. The results of 
the model fitting for the research variables are presented in Table 3. As 
shown in Table 3, the fit indices of the measurement models for the 
research variables reached an acceptable range, indicating that the 
measurement models of the variables align well with the sample data.
We conducted an exploratory factor analysis of the data using SPSS 
21.0. First, the KMO (Kaiser - Meyer - Olkin) measure and Bartlett ’s test of sphericity were performed on the questionnaire data to determine 
whether the study variables were suitable for exploratory factor anal-
ysis. As shown in Table 4, the KMO value of this study was greater than 
0.8, and the result of Bartlett ’s test of sphericity was significant. This 
indicates that the questionnaire data is suitable for exploratory factor 
analysis. Second, principal component analysis was employed to extract 
the common factors with eigenvalues greater than 1. Subsequently, the 
varimax rotation method was used to rotate these common factors to 
obtain the loadings of each measurement item on the common factors. 
As can be seen from Table 4, factor loadings are greater than 0.5, and the 
number of items for each common factor is greater than 2. These items 
effectively reflect the information of the common factors.
With regard to the reliability test, Table 4illustrates that the CR 
coefficients of prospective boundary-spanning search, responsive 
boundary-spanning search, organizational resilience, big data analytic 
capabilities, and open innovation were all greater than 0.9, which is 
greater than the threshold of 0.7. It can therefore be concluded that the 
measurement scales of each research variable demonstrate good com-
posite reliability. The Cronbach ’s α coefficient of each research variable 
exceeded the threshold of 0.7, thereby indicating that the measurement 
scales of the research variables exhibit good internal consistency. The 
measurement scales of prospective boundary-spanning search, respon -
sive boundary-spanning search, organizational resilience, big data an-
alytics capability and open innovation are reliable.
Three validities tests were conducted in this study. First, confirma -
tory factor analysis was used to test construct validity. The Kaiser- 
Meyer-Olkin (KMO) values for the variables exceeded 0.800, and Bar-
tlett’s test yielded a significance of 0.000, confirming their suitability for 
factor analysis. Second, the average variance extraction (AVE) surpassed 
0.700, indicating strong convergent validity in the questionnaire and an 
ideal model quality. Third, confirmatory factor analysis was performed 
to assess discriminant validity. The results yielded a reasonable fit. As 
displayed in Table 5, compared the five-factor model with other factor

4.3. Mediating effects test
To examine the mediating role of organizational resilience in the

Furthermore, this study utilized the Medcurve macro program to

4.4. Moderating effects test
To investigate the moderating influence of BDAC on the inverted U-

in two scenarios. Firstly, in the curvilinear relationship Y β0 β1X 
β2X2 β3XZ β4X2Z β5Z, the direction of the curve ’s shift —whether 
to the right or left—is determined by the sign of β1β4 - β2β3. Secondly, the 
steepness or flatness of the curve is judged based on the positivity or 
negativity of β4.
As evident from the results presented in Model 10 and Model 11 in 
Table 7, BDAC significantly moderate the inverted U-shaped relation -
ship between PBS (β 0.394, p D0.001) and RBS (β 0.114, p D0.05) 
with regard to organizational resilience. Specifically, this moderation 
flattens the inverted U-shaped relationship. Furthermore, our calcula -
tions reveal that as BDAC increase, the apex of organizational resilience 
shifts to the right (0.059), whereas the apex of organizational resilience 
shifts to the left (0.003). These findings validate hypotheses H5a and 
H5b(see Fig. 2).

Based on resource-based theory, the nonlinear relationships between 
prospective and responsive boundary-spanning search and open inno-
vation are clarified. Our findings reveal that both prospective and 
responsive boundary-spanning searches exhibit an inverted U-shaped 
influence on open innovation. In other words, a moderate level of both 
types of searches is most beneficial for firms to successfully engage in 
open innovation. This discovery aligns with previous studies by Yang 
et al. (2021) and Zhu et al. (2023) , who also observed that the rela-
tionship between firms ’ boundary-spanning search and innovation is not 
linear. Our study elucidates the dualistic nature of prospective 
boundary-spanning search and responsive boundary-spanning search as 
pivotal antecedents of open innovation. Furthermore, it sheds light on 
the intrinsic motivational mechanism underlying boundary-spanning 
search and its impact on open innovation.
Utilizing dynamic capabilities theory, we clarify the nonlinear effects 
of both prospective and responsive boundary-spanning searches on 
organizational resilience. Our research demonstrates that both types of 
searches exhibit an inverted U-shaped influence on organizational 
resilience. Specifically, a moderate level of both searches is most 
conducive for firms to build and maintain organizational resilience. This 
finding is consistent with Wang et al. (2023) , who similarly concluded 
that prospective boundary-spanning search has an inverted U-shaped 
effect on both prospective and improvisational resilience. Furthermore, 
our study reveals that both types of boundary-spanning searches play a 
significant role in enhancing organizational resilience, thereby 
expanding the current understanding of organizational resilience.
Based on dynamic capabilities theory, the mediating role of organi -
zational resilience in the relationship between prospective and respon -
sive boundary-spanning search and open innovation is elucidated. Our 
findings reveal that organizational resilience partially mediates the as-
sociation between both prospective and responsive boundary-spanning 
searches and open innovation. This discovery aligns with the research 
conducted by Wang et al. (2023) , who determined that organizational 
resilience serves as a mediator between prospective boundary-spanning 
search and green innovation. Our study further delves into the formation 
mechanism of open innovation, validates the crucial role of organiza -
tional resilience in fostering open innovation, and augments the existing 
research on the antecedents and consequences of organizational 
resilience.
Drawing on dynamic capabilities theory, the moderating influence of 
big data analytics capability on the nonlinear relationship between 
boundary-spanning search and organizational resilience is clarified. Our 
research demonstrates that high big data analytics capability flattens the 
inverted U-shaped relationship between both prospective and respon -
sive boundary-spanning searches and organizational resilience. This 
study illuminates the moderating effect of big data analytics capability 
on organizational resilience, offering deeper insights into the role of 
prospective and responsive boundary-spanning search in enhancing 
organizational resilience at different levels of big data analytics capa-
bility. The study reveals the intrinsic mechanism of prospective and 
responsive boundary-spanning search on open innovation through 
organizational resilience and big data analytical capability in the digital 
era, and provides theoretical support for smart manufacturing firms to 
carry out open innovation.
5.3. Managerial implications
First, firms must strike a balance when conducting boundary- 
spanning search. As the saying goes, "too much is as bad as too little." 
The scope of prospective and responsive boundary-spanning searches 
should be limited to a reasonable range in order to optimize their 
beneficial impacts and mitigate potential adverse effects. Managers 
should distribute resources between prospective and responsive 
boundary-spanning searches based on the firm’s stage of development S. Jin et al. 13 and strategic objectives. For start-ups or firms pursuing breakthrough 
innovations, increasing investments in prospective boundary-spanning 
search can help explore new technologies, markets, and partnerships, 
thereby creating opportunities for new development avenues. For 
mature firms, balancing prospective and responsive searches is essential. 
Managers should ensure a certain degree of foresight when increasing 
responsive search to address market demands effectively while quickly 
adjusting resource allocation to meet changing conditions. Additionally, 
those responsible for managing smart manufacturing companies must 
maintain a balance between prospective and responsive boundary- 
spanning searches. Regularly evaluating the efficiency and effective -
ness of these searches can dynamically adjust search strategies in 
response to market changes or competitive pressures. Prospective 
boundary-spanning search enables firms to identify emerging opportu -
nities and trends, while responsive boundary-spanning search allows 
firms to respond to immediate threats and market changes. Excessive 
reliance on either type of search may result in missed opportunities or 
delayed responses. Therefore, managers should develop a unified 
strategy combining prospective and responsive boundary-spanning 
searches to achieve a balance between innovation efficiency and the 
technological advantages of featured products. A comprehensive 
boundary-spanning search program ensures that smart manufacturing 
firms are fully informed about external environmental changes and 
market dynamics. Conversely, well-developed search system can 
enhance access to knowledge and information while allowing adjust -
ments in search priorities based on real-time market feedback or 
changes.
Second, boundary-spanning search play a crucial role in enhancing 
organizational resilience by promoting open innovation, which enables 
firms to adapt effectively to dynamic market conditions. Organizational 
resilience refers to the ability of an organization to quickly recover from 
internal and external challenges while maintaining or improving its 
competitive position. Prospective boundary-spanning search involves 
exploring new technologies, emerging markets, and potential partner -
ship avenues beyond current boundaries, helping firms identify oppor -
tunities and threats in the changing environment. By integrating these 
discoveries with existing resources through targeted strategies, firms can 
build organizational resilience by adapting to external challenges. At the 
same time, prospective boundary-spanning search aids in adjusting in-
ternal strategies to better align with market dynamics. Responsive 
boundary-spanning search complements this process by enabling firms 
to monitor competitors’ successes and failures in technological inno-
vation and market strategies, allowing them to respond swiftly without 
deterring internal development. By learning from best practices of in-
dustry leaders, firms optimize their resource allocation and operational 
processes, enhancing efficiency and flexibility. This includes improving 
supply chain management or production optimization to reduce costs 
and improve operational efficiency. Together, prospective boundary- 
spanning search and responsive boundary-spanning search help firms 
continuously integrate, build, and reconfigure resources through various 
mechanisms, enabling them to perceive, respond to, and adapt to 
environmental changes effectively. In doing so, firms can enhance 
organizational resilience, which is critical for maintaining competitive 
advantages and achieving sustainable growth in complex and volatile 
markets. To proactively build organizational resilience, managers 
should take steps such as providing systematic training for employees, 
covering skills like interdisciplinary teamwork, rapid learning, and 
adaptation to change. Additionally, optimizing the organizational 
structure and establishing a flexible operating mechanism can facilitate 
the rapid adjustment of resource allocation when market conditions 
change. In this way, developing flexible contingency plans can ensure a 
firm’s capacity to maintain stable operations in an uncertain environ -
ment, thereby promoting open innovation.
Finally, in the digital age, smart manufacturing firms must leverage 
big data technologies to enhance decision-making, resource allocation, 
and real-time monitoring of boundary-spanning search activities. By analyzing market trends, customer demand, and internal firms’ data 
comprehensively, managers can better assess the effectiveness of 
different search strategies and adjust their focus accordingly. For 
instance, analyzing market trends and customer feedback can provide a 
deeper understanding of market dynamics, enabling firms to develop 
more targeted and effective management strategies. Smart 
manufacturing firms need to approach the implementation of big data 
analytics capabilities in several ways. For instance, they must establish a 
comprehensive data management system that includes data collection, 
storage, organization, and analysis, ensuring data quality and security. 
Moreover, they should introduce advanced big data analysis technolo -
gies and tools, such as machine learning and artificial intelligence, to 
improve efficiency and accuracy in data processing. In addition, they 
need to cultivate professional big data analysis talents and establish data 
analysis teams to provide the necessary support for implementing these 
capabilities. By integrating these approaches, smart manufacturing 
firms can harness big data analytics to drive innovation, optimize op-
erations, and achieve sustainable growth in the digital age.
In particular, Haier, a prominent entity within the home appliance 
sector, not only persists in pioneering advancements within the domain 
of home appliances but also diversifies its operations to encompass the 
field of smart homes. Firstly, Haier acquires cutting-edge technologies 
such as the Internet of Things and big data through prospective 
boundary-spanning searches, applies these technologies to home appli -
ances through independent research and development or collaboration 
with Tencent Cloud and Huaxida, and jointly develops smart home so-
lutions to realize open innovation. Concurrently, Haier is also proac -
tively developing an industrial Internet platform to achieve the 
transformation from a home appliance manufacturer to an Internet of 
Things eco-brand. Secondly, Haier has conducted responsive boundary- 
spanning search in the field of smart home appliances and launched a 
series of innovative products, including smart refrigerators and smart 
washing machines. These products not only satisfy consumers’ demand 
for smart home appliances, but also facilitate the intelligent develop -
ment of the home appliance industry. Consequently, Haier has consis -
tently expanded its business scope and market reach through 
prospective and responsive boundary-spanning search, thereby 
enhancing the firm’s risk resilience and market competitiveness. This 
resilience enables Haier to promptly adjust its strategic orientation to 
response to shifts in the external environment, and provide uninter -
rupted and accurate services to users throughout the entire process of 
demand identification, order channels, marketing and sales, order 
management, service operation and maintenance, and user segmenta -
tion. This rapid response capability enables Haier to maintain a leading 
position in fierce market competition.
5.4. Limitations and future research
Firstly, our study employs cross-sectional data to investigate the 
intricate relationship between prospective and responsive boundary- 
spanning search and open innovation. However, future research can 
use longitudinal data and experimental methods to enhance causal 
reasoning, and delve deeper into whether the impact of prospective and 
responsive boundary-spanning search and open innovation varies at 
different stages of a firm’s lifecycle. This approach will offer a more 
nuanced understanding of how these factors evolve and interact over 
time.
Secondly, while we emphasized the significance of big data analytics 
capability in explaining the relationship between prospective and 
responsive boundary-spanning search, organizational resilience, and 
open innovation, our analysis did not delve into its disaggregated di-
mensions. Future studies could categorize big data analytics capability 
into two primary components: big data technical capabilities and big 
data application capabilities. Such an analysis would provide a more 
comprehensive understanding of how big data analytics capability in-
fluence organizational open innovation. Furthermore, the moderating S.
