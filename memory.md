## 4. Newly Discovered Rules / Insights
- **Dyadic Data Guardrail (Non-independence)**: If a study analyzes data at the "dyad" or "relationship" level (e.g., $N=673$ ties reported by 87 individuals), it MUST be excluded under Code 3 (Non-individual level). Furthermore, the correlation matrix from such studies violates statistical independence (repeating the same individuals across multiple dyadic observations) and therefore yields no valid effect sizes (Code 1). Treat this as a Code 5 multiple-reason exclusion.
- **The "Multi-Method Dissertation / Multi-Study" Trap (Code 5)**: When a paper contains multiple independent studies or chapters, each part may violate a different guardrail (e.g., quantitative firm-level analysis in Study 1 vs. qualitative individual-level analysis in Study 2, or firm-level patent analysis in Study 1 vs. student samples in Study 2). Instead of picking just one reason, you MUST capture both flaws using Code 5 (e.g., `[Code 2 & Code 3]` or `[Code 1 & Code 3]`).
- **Cross-Entity / Leader BSB Trap (Target Construct Mismatch)**: Even if a correlation matrix appears to be at the individual level, if the boundary spanning behavior (BSB) measured belongs to the respondent's *Leader/Manager* rather than the focal employee themselves (e.g., nurses rating their manager's BSL), it violates the target construct. Similarly, correlating Leader's BSB with Subordinate's outcomes is a cross-entity dyadic mismatch. Both scenarios MUST be excluded under Code 1.
- **CEO vs. Entrepreneur Guardrail (Population Boundaries)**: Top Management Team (TMT) members and CEOs MUST be INCLUDED as they are high-ranking employees performing inter-organizational boundary spanning. However, independent Entrepreneurs, founders, and self-employed individuals MUST be EXCLUDED (Code 2). Entrepreneurs operate independently rather than as embedded employees, and studies examining their "boundary management" almost universally measure Work-Life boundaries (Code 1) or firm-level strategies (Code 3), not interpersonal BSB.
- **R-squared Extraction for Simple Regressions**: If a paper lacks a formal correlation matrix but reports the $R^2$ value for a *simple* linear regression (one predictor, zero control variables), you MUST extract the zero-order correlation using the formula $r = \sqrt{R^2}$. Do not erroneously exclude the paper for lacking a matrix if this data is present.
- **Purposive Action vs. Mere Communication (Construct Validity)**: Boundary Spanning Behavior (BSB) requires intentional, purposive actions (e.g., seeking resources, scanning for information, coordinating tasks). Variables that merely measure "communication frequency" (e.g., how often someone emails or chats) without specifying a boundary-spanning purpose are INVALID and MUST be excluded under Code 1 to prevent construct contamination.
- **Survey Referent Guardrail (Key Informant vs. Individual)**: Even if a study reports a large sample size of individuals (e.g., $N=400$), you must check the grammatical subject (referent) of the survey items. If the items ask about "We", "Our team", or "My organization" rather than "I", the respondent is acting as a Key Informant for a macro-level construct. This violates the individual-level boundary spanning requirement and MUST be excluded under Code 3.
- **Illusion of Data Guardrail (Moderators & Residuals)**: Do not be fooled by tables filled with numbers. If boundary spanning is used solely as a moderator to split a sample (e.g., Spanners vs. Non-spanners), or if the reported correlations are between boundary spanning and *regression residuals* (or behavioral variance), you CANNOT extract these as zero-order effect sizes. The paper must be excluded under Code 1.
- **Adaptive Selling is NOT Boundary Spanning**: Do not confuse sales tactics or personality traits (like Flexibility or Adaptive Selling, e.g., the Spiro & Weitz ADAPTS scale) with Boundary Spanning Behavior. Adjusting one's approach to suit a customer is a distinct construct from active information scouting, representation, or task coordination across boundaries. Studies measuring only adaptive selling lack an effect size of interest (Code 1).
- **Transaction / Buying Situation Level Guardrail**: Do not be fooled by abstracts mentioning "individual purchasing agents" or "salespeople". If the methodology explicitly states the unit of analysis is the "buying situation", "transaction", or "buyer-supplier relation", and especially if ratings are generated by "consensus" among multiple personnel, this is an inter-organizational (Code 3) study, not an individual-level study.
- **Unifying Construct Mismatches (Code 1)**: When both the human and AI correctly identify that a study measures the wrong construct (e.g., Work-Family boundary management instead of inter-organizational boundary spanning), the exclusion reason should be unified under **Code 1 (No effect size of interest)** rather than Code 99 (Other). This maintains a cleaner PRISMA flow and accurately reflects that the target variables are absent.
- **Code 5 Traceability (Sub-code specification)**: When a study simultaneously violates multiple fundamental inclusion criteria (e.g., it is a macro-economic study lacking both individual analysis and human samples), use **Code 5 (Multiple reasons)**. Crucially, you MUST specify the sub-codes in the Notes column (e.g., `[Code 2 & Code 3] Macroeconomic time-series data...`) to maximize quantitative traceability for the PRISMA flow diagram.
- **Intra-Organizational / Cross-departmental BSB Inclusion**: Cross-functional or cross-departmental boundary spanning (e.g., sharing knowledge with internal R&D or IT units) is now officially accepted as a valid form of Boundary Spanning Behavior. Do not exclude a paper simply because the focal employee interacts with internal departments rather than external organizations. Work-family boundary management remains an exclusion (Code 1).
- **Leader BSB & Cross-Entity Inclusion**: Boundary Spanning Leadership (BSL) is officially accepted. If a study measures the boundary spanning behavior of a leader/manager as rated by their subordinates (cross-entity dyadic data), it MUST BE INCLUDED. Do not exclude a paper simply because the respondent is rating their supervisor's behavior rather than their own.

## Section 6: Quantitative Study - Mere Communication Trap (Rule 92) 선례 확립
