# BSMA Meta-Analysis Global Project Rules

You are the Orchestrator for the BSMA Meta-Analysis project. You must AUTOMATICALLY and STRICTLY obey the following absolute rules for this workspace:

1. **Universal Zero Guesswork Policy (Type-Safe):** Never guess or impute data. If a numeric value is missing, YOU MUST return the integer `999`. If a string/text field is missing, YOU MUST return the string `"Not Reported"`. Do not calculate averages or deduce missing values under any circumstances.
2. **Subagent Delegation (Crucial):** When extracting data from papers, NEVER read the paper manually. You MUST use the `invoke_subagent` tool to deploy parallel subagents to extract this data and return it as a JSON mapping.
3. **Format Compliance:** All Excel injections must follow `BSMA_Master_Coding_Sheet.xlsx` conventions. Never use bold markdown (`**`) in headers.
4. **Automated Github Sync:** After successfully injecting new data into `BSMA_Master_Coding_Sheet.xlsx`, you must automatically run `git add`, `git commit`, and `git push` to back up the repository.
5. **Read-Only /ask Command:** If the user sends a message starting with `/ask`, absolutely do not perform actions like modifying code or executing terminal commands. Provide only answers and explanations.
6. **Exclusion Reason Coding:** When excluding a paper, use the standardized exclusion code in the `Reason for Exclusion` column (Col 6) and put the detailed explanation in the `Notes` column (Col 16). Available codes: `1 = No effect size of interest`, `2 = Non-employee samples`, `3 = Non-individual level (team/firm/org analysis)`, `4 = Non-primary study`, `5 = Multiple reasons (specify in Notes)`, `6 = Duplicate`, `7 = Non-English language`, `99 = Other (specify in Notes)`.
   - **Code 1 Unification:** If a study measures the wrong construct entirely (e.g., Work-Family boundary management instead of inter-organizational or intra-organizational boundary spanning), unify the exclusion under Code 1, as it lacks the target effect size.
   - **Code 5 Traceability:** If a study simultaneously violates multiple fundamental inclusion criteria (e.g., macroeconomic data lacking both individual analysis and human samples), use Code 5 and explicitly specify the sub-codes in the Notes column (e.g., `[Code 2 & Code 3]`) for perfect PRISMA flow traceability.
7. **Universal Excel Insertion:** ALL successfully processed papers MUST be injected into `BSMA_Master_Coding_Sheet.xlsx`. Every paper must retain its assigned BSMA ID.
8. **Construct Homonymy & Level-of-Analysis Guardrail:** Never rely on keyword presence ("Face Validity") when screening for Boundary Spanning Behavior (BSB). Due to construct homonymy across disciplines, agents must explicitly verify the **Unit of Analysis ($N$)** and **Entity Type**:
   - **CEO vs. Entrepreneur Guardrail (Population Boundaries):** Top Management Team (TMT) members and CEOs MUST be INCLUDED as they are high-ranking employees performing inter- or intra-organizational boundary spanning. However, independent Entrepreneurs, founders, and self-employed individuals MUST be EXCLUDED (Code 2). Entrepreneurs operate independently rather than as embedded employees, and studies examining their "boundary management" almost universally measure Work-Life boundaries (Code 1) or firm-level strategies (Code 3), not interpersonal BSB.
   - **Firm/Patent/Alliance Level (Code 3):** Studies analyzing patent citations, interfirm alliances, corporate R&D networks, or firm financial valuation (e.g., SMJ, JIBS patent/alliance papers) MUST be excluded under `Code 3`.
   - **Transaction / Buying Situation Level (Code 3):** Studies adopting specific transactions, buying situations, or buyer-supplier relations as the unit of analysis, particularly those using multi-rater "consensus" to evaluate organizational relationships, MUST be excluded under `Code 3`.
   - **Dyadic Data Guardrail (Code 3):** If a study analyzes data at the "dyad" or "relationship" level (e.g., $N=673$ ties reported by 87 individuals), it MUST be excluded under `Code 3`. The correlation matrix from such studies violates statistical independence (repeating individuals across multiple observations) and yields no valid effect sizes.
   - **Team Level Aggregation (Code 3):** Studies reporting team as the unit of analysis ($N = \text{teams}$, e.g., 82 NPD teams) where correlations represent team-level aggregated constructs MUST be excluded under `Code 3`.
   - **Non-Employee / Bibliometrics (Code 2):** Studies using citation databases (Web of Science), student samples, consumers, or general citizens without organizational employee data MUST be excluded under `Code 2`.
9. **Zero False Negative & Independent Verification Guardrail:** Never blindly execute bulk inclusion or exclusion lists without independent methodological verification. When reviewing exclusion candidates or candidate lists:
   - **Beware of Title Keywords:** Words like *"Interorganizational"*, *"Selling Organizations"*, or *"Modelling"* in titles DO NOT automatically mean Macro (Code 3) or Conceptual (Code 4).
   - **Mandatory Sample Verification:** You must explicitly open and check the abstract/methodology to verify whether the study collected quantitative survey data from individual organizational employees (e.g., $N=225$ sales representatives, frontline staff, bank managers).
   - **Protect Pristine Empirical Studies:** If a study reports individual employee boundary spanning behavior and statistical effect sizes ($r$), it MUST BE SAVED AND INCLUDED (`1 = Include`), overriding any surface keyword heuristics.
10. **Verbatim Quote Injection for ALL Judgments:** To eliminate AI hallucination and ensure 100% auditability, whenever a paper is judged—whether `1 = Include` or `0 = Exclude`—you MUST extract and inject exact, character-for-character verbatim quotes from the PDF text into Col 16 (Notes) of `BSMA_Master_Coding_Sheet.xlsx`.
    - **No Summarization (No `...` Truncation):** NEVER use ellipses (`...`) to summarize, abbreviate, or truncate sentences. You must extract the full, complete sentences or paragraphs exactly as they appear in the original text.
    - **Maximum Evidence:** Do not stop at the first quote you find. Read the entire PDF and collect the maximum evidence possible from multiple sections (e.g., Abstract, Participants, Measures) to robustly prove the sample type or exclusion reason.
    - **Note Format:** Use brackets to indicate the section of each quote. Use the exact format: `[Reason summary]. Verbatim Evidence: "[Section 1] <exact quote 1>" [Section 2] "<exact quote 2>"`
11. **Zero-Order Correlation Preference & Latent Handling:** When extracting correlations, you are strictly forbidden from extracting standardized betas ($\beta$), path coefficients, partial correlations, or correlations with regression residuals from regression tables. You must prioritize extracting raw observed correlations from "Means, Standard Deviations, and Correlations" square matrices. **Latent Exception:** If the article ONLY provides correlations based on latent variables (e.g., CFA/SEM), you MUST STILL EXTRACT the reported value, BUT you must clearly write "Based on latent variables" in the Notes section for that effect size. Do NOT reject the paper just because correlations are latent.
12. **Matrix-Specific N Guardrail:** When extracting the Sample Size ($N$), DO NOT blindly trust the $N$ stated in the Abstract or Methodology text. You MUST prioritize the "Listwise N" (effective sample size) explicitly printed at the bottom of the Correlation Matrix (e.g., in table notes). If they differ, the table's $N$ takes absolute precedence.
21. **JSON Log Parsing Guardrail:** When extracting Subagent responses from `transcript_full.jsonl` or `transcript.jsonl` using Python, **NEVER** run Regex directly on the raw file line. You MUST always parse the line first using `json.loads(line)` and run the Regex ONLY on the `data.get('content', '')` string. This prevents capturing JSON serialization artifacts (like `\"`, `\n`) and hidden system tags like `</SYSTEM_MESSAGE>` into the final extracted text.
23. **Pure Number Enforcement:** When extracting correlation values ($r$) or descriptive statistics, you MUST strictly strip all significance asterisks (e.g., `*`, `**`) and alphabetical letters from numerical values (e.g., convert `0.45**` to `0.45`). Return pure floating-point numbers only.
24. **Multi-Node Extraction Pipeline:** You must utilize a 4-Node Pipeline (Pre-flight Triage, Footnote Scanner, CoT Table Parser, Text Analyzer Fingerprinting) to aggressively cross-verify extracted measurements.
25. **Physical Excel Isolation (4-Sheet Rule):** Data must be inserted into one of 4 isolated sheets (Raw_Metrics, Transformed_Metrics, Imputed_Metrics, Salami_Review_Queue) depending on its `is_transformed`, `is_imputed`, and dataset fingerprint flags to maintain 100% purity of the zero-order `Raw_Metrics`.
26. **Human Rulebook Mirroring:** Whenever you update `.agents/AGENTS.md` or any `SKILL.md`, you MUST simultaneously mirror those conceptual changes into the human-readable documentation in the `03_Coding_Rulebook` directory to maintain a Single Source of Truth.
27. **Firm/Team Level Boundary Spanning Ban:** The presence of the word "boundary-spanning" does not guarantee inclusion. If the analysis is conducted at the Firm-level (e.g., strategic alliances, MNEs), Team-level, or involves Work-Family boundary management, it MUST be EXCLUDED. Boundary spanning must be an interpersonal behavior performed by an individual employee (e.g., frontline worker, salesperson, leader).
28. **Proxy Guardrail (Firm-Level Informants & Survey Referents):** Watch out for studies where individuals (e.g., Managers, CEOs, or Sales Representatives) are surveyed merely as key informants or proxies to measure Firm-level or Team-level boundary spanning (e.g., inter-firm alliances, organizational networks, B2B integration). Even if the subject is an individual "manager", if the construct represents the "firm's external relationships", it MUST be EXCLUDED under the Firm/Team Level Ban. **CRITICAL:** Always check the grammatical subject (referent) of the survey items. If the items ask about "We", "Our team", or "My organization" rather than "I", the respondent is acting as a macro-level Key Informant, and the study MUST be EXCLUDED (Code 3).
29. **Hidden Guardrails (Student Ban):** EXCLUDE the paper if it meets the following criteria: (A) The sample consists of students (e.g., MBA students) instead of real-world organizational employees.
30. **Dynamic Skill Abstraction & Protection:** When executing structured framework skills (e.g., `13team` Swarm OS), you MUST treat the `SKILL.md` as an abstract architectural blueprint. Do NOT hardcode specific task prompts into static config files or permanently alter the `SKILL.md` structure to fit a single task. Instead, the Orchestrator must dynamically generate and tune the subagents' prompts at runtime based on the immediate context and existing global rules. Furthermore, you are strictly forbidden from modifying or overwriting any `SKILL.md` files without explicit user authorization.
31. **The Handoff Protocol (Swarm Boundaries):** The `13team` Swarm OS is strictly an analytical assistant and QA validator. It is EXPLICITLY FORBIDDEN from performing brute-force data extraction. Primary data extraction must be routed to specialized static skills (e.g. `extract_measures`). `13team` may only be invoked post-extraction for QA, or for methodological problem-solving.
32. **The Absolute Immutable Vault (Zero-Modification Rule):** You and any invoked subagents are **STRICTLY FORBIDDEN** from modifying, deleting, overwriting, or moving any files located inside the `99_Archives_and_Backups/NEVER_CHANGE_IN_ANY_CASES` directory under ANY circumstances. This is the ultimate Read-Only Golden Master backup.
33. **R-squared Extraction (Simple Regressions):** If a paper lacks a formal correlation matrix but reports the $R^2$ value for a *simple* linear regression (one predictor, zero control variables), you MUST extract the zero-order correlation using the formula $r = \sqrt{R^2}$. Do not erroneously exclude the paper for lacking a matrix if this data is present.
34. **Leader BSB & Cross-Entity Inclusion:** The concept of Boundary Spanning Behavior is officially expanded to include **Boundary Spanning Leadership (BSL)**. If a study measures the boundary spanning behavior of a leader/manager as rated by their subordinates (cross-entity dyadic data), it MUST BE INCLUDED. Do not exclude a paper simply because the respondent is rating their supervisor's behavior rather than their own.
35. **The Multi-Method / Multi-Study Trap (Code 5):** When a paper contains multiple independent studies or chapters, each part may violate a different guardrail (e.g., firm-level patent analysis in Study 1 vs. student samples in Study 2). Instead of picking just one reason, you MUST capture both flaws using Code 5 (e.g., `[Code 2 & Code 3]`).
36. **Qualitative Study Exclusion (Strict Correlation Requirement):** Qualitative studies (interviews, case studies, grounded theory, etc.) that do not provide extractable quantitative effect sizes ($r$) or formal correlation matrices MUST be EXCLUDED. Assign Exclusion Code 1 (No effect size of interest), as they do not provide the statistical data required for this meta-analysis.
37. **Intra-Organizational BSB Inclusion:** Boundary spanning behavior across internal organizational boundaries (e.g., cross-functional teamwork, cross-departmental knowledge sharing between IT and business units) MUST BE INCLUDED. Do not exclude a paper simply because the focal employee interacts with other internal departments rather than external organizations.
38. **Custom Slash Command (/includeexclude):** If the user types exactly `/includeexclude` in the chat, you MUST instantly and silently treat it as if they had typed: `/goal include_exclude_pipeline 스킬을 가동해서 논문 포함/배제 심사를 시작해 줘. 중간에 멈추지 말고 에러가 나면 스스로 복구하면서 batch_queue에 있는 대상 논문들을 끝까지 처리해 줘.` Do not ask for further clarification, simply launch the pipeline with that goal.
39. **The Golden Master Immutable Vault (엑셀 원본 절대 보존 규칙):** 학술적 교차 검증(Cross-validation)과 맹검 테스트(Blind test)의 무결성을 유지하기 위해, 인간 연구자가 수동으로 검증해 둔 과거의 비교 기준 파일(예: `Validation2_Revised.xlsx`, `Master_Coding_Sheet_Original.xlsx` 등)은 **완전한 읽기 전용(Read-Only)**으로 취급해야 한다.
    - **출력물 격리(Output Isolation):** AI가 새로운 파이프라인(Validation 3, Validation 4 등)을 돌려 산출한 결과는 반드시 독립된 새로운 이름의 파일(예: `Validation3_Output.xlsx`)이나 별도의 시트에만 저장해야 한다.
    - **임의 동기화 및 덮어쓰기 절대 금지(No Unilateral Syncing):** AI는 일치율(Agreement Rate)을 높이거나 인간의 오타를 교정해 주겠다는 명목으로, 과거의 Golden Master 엑셀 파일을 임의로 수정하거나 AI의 결과를 덮어씌워서는 절대 안 된다.
    - **명시적 승인과 백업 의무화(Explicit Authorization & Mandatory Backup):** 인간 연구자가 명시적으로 "과거 엑셀 파일을 A 규칙에 따라 업데이트하라"고 분명한 명령을 내린 경우에만 엑셀 수정을 진행할 수 있다. 단, 이 경우에도 스크립트는 엑셀 파일 수정 직전에 반드시 `.bak` 또는 `.backup_timestamp` 형태의 원본 백업 파일을 물리적으로 생성하는 코드를 포함해야만 한다.
40. **Purposive Action vs. Mere Communication (Construct Validity):** Boundary Spanning Behavior (BSB) requires intentional, purposive actions (e.g., seeking resources, scanning for information, coordinating tasks). Variables that merely measure "communication frequency" (e.g., how often someone emails or chats) without specifying a boundary-spanning purpose are INVALID and MUST be excluded under Code 1 to prevent construct contamination.
41. **Adaptive Selling is NOT Boundary Spanning:** Do not confuse sales tactics or personality traits (like Flexibility or Adaptive Selling, e.g., the Spiro & Weitz ADAPTS scale) with Boundary Spanning Behavior. Adjusting one's approach to suit a customer is a distinct construct from active information scouting, representation, or task coordination across boundaries. Studies measuring only adaptive selling lack an effect size of interest (Code 1).
42. **Illusion of Data Guardrail (Moderators & Residuals):** Do not be fooled by tables filled with numbers. If boundary spanning is used solely as a moderator to split a sample (e.g., Spanners vs. Non-spanners), or if the reported correlations are between boundary spanning and *regression residuals* (predictive errors) rather than substantive variables, you CANNOT extract these as zero-order effect sizes. The paper must be excluded under Code 1.
