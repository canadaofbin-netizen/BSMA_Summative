  Discussion
  There are certain factors that contribute to group performance. Theory of resource-based
  view suggests the use of group resources (internal) in such a combination that these could
  create a synergy effect in order to increase workgroup performance. However, according to
  resource dependence theory, while utilizing group resources group leaders and members
  should bear in mind that certain external resources of the group can influence group internal
  environment both positively and negatively.

  We studied the role of two group-level internal resources toward group performance, i.e.,
  group composition and group membersâ€™ experience. The results of our study confirm that
  both these resources negatively predict group performance. According to Zhan et al. (2015),
  gender-based group composition significantly influences group performance. They have
  observed that males perform better in mix-gender groups and females can perform equally
  whether group comprises all female members or mixed gender. Moreover, males prefer to
  join groups having a mix-gender, while females prefer single-gender groups. Gender
  differences occur on attitude rather than learning achievement. For group formation and
  improved performance, it is required that group members demonstrate mutual interest and
  understanding with each other.
